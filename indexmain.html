ISMIS Webpage Critique

ISMIS has been a persistent issue for years, affecting not only students but also faculty, staff, and department heads. As the university's primary system for enrollment, grade viewing, and other essential academic processes, its performance significantly impacts our daily academic experience.

While ISMIS serves a critical purpose, it suffers from major usability issues. The system is often **slow, unresponsive, and prone to downtime**, making it difficult for students to access important information when needed. These inefficiencies have caused frustration among users who rely on the platform for key academic tasks.

Given its importance, ISMIS **needs a major overhaul**—focusing on improved performance, intuitive navigation, and better responsiveness to meet the growing demands of students and faculty alike.

HTML STRUCTURE

  • Does the website use semantic HTML elements?
  • Is the HTML code well-organized, with proper nesting and indentation?
  • Are there any missing elements that could improve the structure?

CSS STYLING

  • Is the layout visually appealing and consistent across the website?
  • Does the website use responsive design techniques?
  • Are the fonts, colors, and spacing used effectively?

USABILITY

  • Is the website easy to navigate? Are menus and links intuitive?
  • Is the text readable?
  • Are interactive elements functional?

ACCESSIBILITY

  • Are images and multimedia elements accompanied by appropriate alt attributes?
  • Can the website be navigated using a keyboard?
  • Does it follow WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) standards?
